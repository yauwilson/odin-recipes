Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. asagne originated in Italy during the Middle Ages. The oldest transcribed text about lasagna appears in 1282 in the Memoriali Bolognesi ("Bolognesi Memorials"), in which lasagna was mentioned in a poem transcribed by a Bolognese notary;[4][5] while the first recorded recipe was set down in the early 14th-century Liber de Coquina (The Book of Cookery).[6] It bore only a slight resemblance to the later traditional form of lasagne, featuring a fermented dough flattened into thin sheets (lasagne), boiled, sprinkled with cheese and spices, and then eaten with a small pointed stick.[7] Recipes written in the century following the Liber de Coquina recommended boiling the pasta in chicken broth and dressing it with cheese and chicken fat. In a recipe adapted for the Lenten fast, walnuts were recommended.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 oz) package lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (16 oz) jar spaghetti sauce
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 lb shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 lb shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1 pint ricotta cheese

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
  2. Add pasta and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain
  3. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, brown beef and season with salt and pepper; drain.
  5. Stir in spaghetti sauce and garlic and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. In a medium bowl, combine mozzarella, Cheddar and ricotta cheese; stir well.
  7. In a 9x13 inch pan, alternate layers of noodles, meat mixutre and cheese mixture until pan is filled.
  8. Bake in preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.

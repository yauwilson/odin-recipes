Creme Brulee

Description

Crème brûlée or crème brulée, also known as burnt cream or Trinity cream, and virtually identical to the original crema catalana, is a dessert consisting of a rich custard base topped with a layer of hardened caramelized sugar.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp white sugar, divided

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  2. Whisk 3 tbsp sugar and cream in a microwave-safe bowl until well combined.
  3. Heat the mixture in microwave until warm, 1 to 2 minutes, and whisk again to dissolve sugar.
  4. Whisk in egg yolks and vanilla extract until smooth.
  5. Pour cream mixture into 2 ramekins.
  6. Set ramekins into a roasting pan and pour in enough hot water to reach halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until creme desserts are set but still slightly jiggly when shaken, about 50 minutes.
  8. Remove ramekins from hot water and chill in refrigerator until cold, at least 2 hours.
  9. Sprinkle 1 tbsp of sugar evenly over the top of each dessert.
  10. Use a kitchen torch to lightly toast and melt the sugar topping until brown and bubbly, about 30 seconds.
  11. Let the sugar topping cool before serving.
  12. To serve, use a spoon to crack the crisp sugar open to reveal the creamy dessert underneath.
